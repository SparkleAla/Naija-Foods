Afang Soup


Source.
This is popular Nigerian delicacy, native to the Efik tribe of Cross River State,
a soup enjoyed by everyone and can be eaten with variety of swallows including; Semovita, Eba, Fufu or Wheat.
This dish when well prepared is bound to have licking your plates

Ingredients;

  • Assorted Meat
  • Afang leaves (popularly called Okazi in the SouthSouth/East)
  • Water leaf
  • Palm oil
  • Dried Fish
  • Stock fish
  • Periwinkle (preferably with the shell)
  • Ground scotch bonnet Pepper
  • Ground Crayfish
  • Salt and seasoning cubes












Health Benefits

Afang Soup has high dietary fibre, which helps in regulating blood sugar, reducing constipation and aiding weight loss. Okazi leaves have a high quantity of iodine and helps to prevent and treat goiter. It boosts immunity and helps improve the body's natural defences against diseases.

Catfish Peppersoup


Source.
This is another delicious Nigerian dish, native to the people of the South Southern part of Nigeria
Known for it's spicy flavor, it is well enjoyed as a whole meal, with rice or swallow.

Health Benefits

Eating fish is an important source of omega-3 fatty acids and minerals. These essential nutrients keep our heart and brain healthy. The spicy flavor from peppersoup also burns fat by triggering hhan increase in body temperature,which causes fat to be burned and expelled through sweat.

Ingredients;

  • Catfish
  • Fresh Pepper, onion and tomatoes (blend together)
  • Scent leaf
  • Palm oil
  • Salt
  • Stock seasoning and spices









Recipe for Catfish Peppersoup :

  1. Wash catfish with hot water till slime free and put in a clean pot.
  2. Add your spice, blended mix and other ingredients.
  3. Cover the pot and allow to cook for about 30-50minutes.
Your spicy catfish peppersoup is ready!!!!
**Best served hot with boiled rice.

Edikang Ikong Soup


Source.
Edikang ikong soup is vegetable soup that originated among the Efik people of Crossriver and
Akwaibom state in South Southern Nigeria. This Nigerian soup recipe is nourishing in every sense of the word.

Ingredients;

  • 1kg pumpkin leaves
  • 600g beef, kanda(cow skin), shaki and dry fish
  • Pepper, salt and ground crayfish
  • 200ml of palm oil
  • 1 cup of periwinkle(shelled)
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2-3 stock cubes









Health Benefits

Edikang Ikong Soup is also high dietary fibre, which helps in regulating blood sugar, reducing constipation and aiding weight loss.It also contains lot of proteins and minerals which is good for the immune system. It also helps improve the body's natural defences against diseases.

Recipe for Edikang Ikong Soup :

  1. Wash and cut the pumpkin and water leaves into tiny pieces.
  2. Put them in separate sieves to drain out all the water.
  3. Cut the kanda into small pieces.
  4. Cook the beef, kanda and the dry fish with the 2 bulbs of diced onions.
  5. Add the stock cubes with a little quantity of water.
  6. When the meat is done to your taste, add a generous amount of
    palm oil, with the crayfish and pepper and leave to boil for about 10 minutes.
  7. Add the periwinkle and water leaves and cook for another 3-5 minutes.
  8. Now add the pumpkin leaves and salt to taste.
  9. Stir the contents of the pot very well and turn off the heat.
  10. Cover the pot and leave to stand for about 4 minutes.
That's it, your rich Edikang Ikong Soup is ready!!

*The palm oil serves as the liquid in the Edikang ikong soup.
**Best served with Garri(Eba), Semolina fufu, Amala, Cassava fufu or Pounded yam.

Yam and Egg Sauce


Source.
While this dish can not be traced to any ethnicity in Nigeria,
it is eaten and enjoyed by all. Yam is mostly grown in the Northern
part of Nigeria amongs't other regions.

Health Benefits

Boiled yam and egg sauce can offer a range of health benefits. Yam is high in fiber and low in fat, making it a good source of complex carbohydrates. It also contains essential vitamins and minerals such as vitamins C and B6, potassium, and manganese. Eggs, on the other hand, are an excellent source of protein, as well as important nutrients such as iron, vitamin D, and choline.
The combination of yam and egg sauce can provide a balanced and nutritious meal that can help promote overall good health. However, it's important to note that the specific health benefits can vary depending on the ingredients and cooking method used for the egg sauce.

Ingredients;

  • 1 large yam
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder











Recipe for Yam and Egg Sauce:

  1. Peel the yam and cut into your desired size.
  2. Rinse with water and put in a pot of salted boiling water.
  3. Boil for 20-25 minutes until the yam is soft.
  4. Heat up the vegetable oil in a pan and sauté the onions and tomatoes until they are soft.
  5. Whisk the eggs in a bowl and add the thyme, garlic powder, curry powder, and salt to the eggs.
  6. Pour the egg mixture into the pan with the sautéed onions and cook for 3 minutes.
